Viscoelastic properties of adhesive soft particles Glasses




This study aims at investigating the rheology and flow behavior of concentrated suspensions of adhesive soft particles. An important feature that distinguishes the adhesive soft particles from other types of soft particles is their ability to exert short-range adhesive interactions due to the hydrophobe bond-forming and breaking across the particle surfaces. My goal is to predict the linear and nonlinear viscoelastic properties and flow behavior of adhesive soft particle suspensions. This is an ongoing project, and we will have several published articles in few months.




Model-based optimal design of experiments with noninvertible fisher information matrix




In this project, I have developed and compared three approaches for handling a noninvertible Fisher Information Matrix (FIM) when designing sequential model-based (e.g., A, D, and V-optimal) experiments. Proposed approaches for dealing with this problem include i) a "leave out" approach, where problematic parameters are identified and left out of the design problem (LO approach), ii,) a pseudo-inverse approach where the Moore Penrose pseudo inverse is used (PI approach), iii) a simple Bayesian approach that adds prior parameter knowledge to the FIM when designing experiments and therefore making it inventible. This study was funded by Eli Lilly pharmaceutical company and its outcomes are published in Industrial & Engineering Chemistry Research , Computers & Chemical Engineering , and AIChE Journal.
